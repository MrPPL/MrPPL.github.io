Is there a Universal Explaination for Economical Inequality?

A casual observer at differences in people wealth will observe that around 80 percent of the wealth of the world is concentrated by 20 percent. Is a logical mathematical explaination for this phenomenon or it is the outcome of policy making. Many people would claim that the second reason it is the most compelling, but I believe it is the outcome of a special structure based on a positive feedback effects from human interactions.

If it is the first time you see Black-Scholes Formula for a European call option, you might be confused where the formula comes from. Originally \[S(T)=s\cdot e^{(r-\frac{1}{2}\sigma^2)(T-t)+\sigma(W_T^Q-W_t^Q)}\]

Proof using Risk Neutral Valuation Formula

\[ c(t,s)=e^{-r(T-t)} E_{t,s}^Q[max{S(T)-K,0}]=e^{-r(T-t)} E_{t,s}^Q[(S(T)-K)1_{S(T)>K}] = e^{-r(T-t)} E_{t,s}^Q[S(T)1_{S(T)>K}] - e^{-r(T-t)} K \cdot Q(S(T)>K)\] We look at each two terms separately: \[ Q(S(T)>K) = Q(s\cdot e^{(r-\frac{1}{2}\sigma^2)(T-t)+\sigma(W_T^Q-W_t^Q)}>K)=Q(Y>\frac{1}{\sigma \sqrt{T-t}} \bigg(\ln(\frac{K}{s}) - (r-\frac{1}{2}\sigma^2)(T-t) )\bigg)= Q(-Y < d_2(t,s)) =\Phi(d_2(t,s)) \] \[ E_{t,s}^Q[S(T)1_{\{S(T)>K\}}] =E_{t,s}^Q[s\cdot e^{(r-\frac{1}{2}\sigma^2)(T-t)+\sigma(W_T^Q-W_t^Q)}1_{\{s\cdot e^{(r-\frac{1}{2}\sigma^2)(T-t)+\sigma(W_T^Q-W_t^Q)}>K\}}]= s\cdot e^{(r-\frac{1}{2}\sigma^2)(T-t)}E_{t,s}^Q[e^{\sigma \cdot \sqrt{T-t}\cdot Y}1_{\{Y>-d_2(t,s)\}}] \overset{(*)}{=} s\cdot e^{r(T-t)} \Phi(d_1(t,s))\] \begin{align} (*) E_{t,s}^Q[e^{\sigma \cdot \sqrt{T-t}\cdot Y}1_{\{Y>-d_2(t,s)\}}]&=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} \int_{-d_2(t,s)}^\infty e^{\sigma \cdot \sqrt{T-t}\cdot y} e^{-\frac{y^2}{2}} dy\\ &=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}\int_{-d_2(t,s)}^\infty e^{\sigma \cdot \sqrt{T-t}\cdot y-\frac{y^2}{2}-\frac{1}{2}\sigma^2} e^{\frac{\sigma^2}{2}} dy\\ &=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{\frac{\sigma^2}{2}} \int_{-d_2(t,s)}^\infty e^{-\frac{1}{2}(y-\sqrt{T-t}\cdot \sigma)} dy\\ &=e^{\frac{\sigma^2}{2}} Q(X>-d_2(t,s))=Q(X-\sigma\sqrt{T-t}>-d_2(t,s)-\sigma\sqrt{T-t}) \quad where \ X\sim \mathcal{N}(\sigma \sqrt{T-t},1)\\ &=e^{\frac{\sigma^2}{2}} \Phi(d_1(t,s)) \end{align} By above calculations we get: \[ c(t,s)=e^{-r(T-t)} E_{t,s}^Q[S(T)1_{S(T)>K}] - e^{-r(T-t)} K \cdot Q(S(T)>K)=s\Phi(d_1(t,s))-e^{-r(T-t)}\cdot K \Phi(d_2(t,s))) \]
