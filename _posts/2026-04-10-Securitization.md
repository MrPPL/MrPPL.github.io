# Wall street 

To understand the financial credit crisis of 07-09 it is necassary to understand the concept of securitization to understand why the subprime market become so overheated.

The big short is one of my all time favorite movies


One thing that has obsesseb me over the years have been a deep understanding of some of the mechanism for the financial crisis and the credit crush. In this blog I will focus on the morgages backed securities and how securitized was a brilliant financial innovation which could became a bubble driver in the morgage industry

Let's start with the brilliant by securitization we enable access to investors with risk appetite to a diversified portfolio of "idiosyncratic" assets. We separate the credit concern of the originator to only the credit concern of this pool of assets while we benefit of the initial underwriting (albeit paying a fee). In finance we will say we get a more "efficient" market.

The worrisome part is actual the underwriting hence if the originator start to slack on their credit quality of the borrower we could end up with skewed picture of the actual credit risk. A player in this is the rating agencies which gives the issuer and issues rating based on the probability of default hence a bad guidance might lead to bad decision making from blind investors.

The basic issue is one of moral hazard where the initial underwriter have no consequence of issuing bad quality loans since they can be quickly sold of to investors via securitization.
