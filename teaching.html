Teaching Activities as TA

Continuous Time Finance at University of Copenhagen

In this course, the students learn to answer the fundamental questions in option theory 'Under what conditions is the market arbitrage free?' and 'Under what conditions is the market complete?'. To answer these question the students learned the mathematics behind the Fundamental Theorems of Mathematical Finance, stochastic calculus, hedging, risk-neutral pricing and the Martingale pricing theorem in a Black-Scholes model.

Financial Derivatives at Aalborg University Business School

In this course, students will learn about different types of financial derivatives and how they are used to transfer risk. Topics include forwards, futures, and options.

Portfolio Theory

This course covers the principles of portfolio construction and management. Students will learn about modern portfolio theory and portfolio optimization techniques.

Econometrics 2: Statistical Analysis of Econometric Time Series

This course builds on the foundations of econometrics and focuses on the mathematics behind the time series models MA, AR, ARMA, ARIMA, ARCH, and GARCH. In the study of the models the students will learn about stationary processes, autocorrelation, and the pro and cons of the models.

Mathematical Modelling

Introduction to Economics

This course provides an introduction to microeconomics and macroeconomics. Students will learn about the fundamentals of supply and demand, and monetary and fiscal policy.

Introduction to Numerical Analysis

In this course, students will learn about numerical methods for solving mathematical problems. Topics include machine representation of floats, linear algebra, root methods, interpolation, differentiation, and integration.
